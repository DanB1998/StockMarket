Why Invest?

Investing into a stock allows you to take ownership of a small share in a company. Purchasing stocks in companies that gain value means that your stock will also gain value, becoming worth more than when you bought it. You can then sell the stock when you feel that your investment has been worthwhile, or if you change your mind about the company you own stocks in.

However not all companies will gain value after you purchase some of their stocks. Some will stay around the same value and others may decrease slightly, causing you to lose a percentage of your investment. This is where good research and experience can help you, so it is not advised that you jump into the market with a large sum of money and little knowledge.

The Different Types of Investing.

Day Trading

Day trading is when you buy and sell different stocks daily. This usually means you are trying to profit off certain trends happening live and this is known as the riskiest type of trading. In my opinion people should not take up this form of trading as it is similar to gambling and there is zero logic or strategy, just a 50% chance of a stock increasing in price, and a 50% chance of it decreasing in chance.

No long term waiting

Incredible risk, 50/50 trading.

Short Term Trading

Short trading is when you buy and sell stocks, usually on a weekly to monthly basis. This is similar to day trading in the fact that it is on a very short timescale. However short trading can be less risk if you can spot a good opportunity to invest, for example before a company releases a new product. However these opportunities may also be negatively received, and can also drive a stock down as well as up.

Possible to find good opportunities for investment.

Still very risky, short term stock price changes can be hard to predict.

Long Term Trading

Long trading is when you buy stocks and hold them, mainly for 2+ years. This reduces the risk of loss as minor changes in a stocks price on a daily/weekly basis do not effect your investment. Long term trading is predicting where a company will be in years to come, and allows for the natural growth in economy to support the success of your investment demostrated by the graphic above.

Allows you to acheieve exponential growth in investments

Can take 10-20+ years of committed investing to get great financial results

Why Long Term Trading is King.

As explained above, I believe long term trading is the way to go when dealing with the stock market. It offers the least risk and can be extremely financially rewarding if committed to and well looked after. A good, well rounded portfolio will only require a handful of hours per month to look after if properly set up and maintained. The main reason long term trading is so powerful and works as well as it does is because it is exploiting a somewhat 'linear' trend in market growth. This growth allows you to make cumulative gains due to reinvestment. Keeping risk at it's lowest by investing in large, financially strong companies in low risk sectors allows you to achieve this cumulative effect that is the reason long term investment can achieve huge financial reward.
